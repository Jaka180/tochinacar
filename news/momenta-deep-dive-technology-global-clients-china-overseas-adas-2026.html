Executive Summary

Momenta (Beijing Chusudu Technology / Mota Suzhou Technology, HKEX ticker: 06880.HK) was founded in 2016 and is today the leading independent autonomous driving software supplier by urban NOA (Navigate on Autopilot) market share among third-party vendors globally. On July 8, 2026, Momenta officially listed on the Hong Kong Stock Exchange (stock code 06880.HK, W-share structure), pricing its IPO at HK$295.60 per share, opening at HK$301, reaching an intraday high of HK$314.80, and closing at approximately HK$299 — a first-day gain of approximately 2.8% — raising approximately HK$5.89 billion (USD ~7.51 billion).[1][2]

Prior to listing, Momenta’s cumulative installed vehicle count had exceeded 1 million units, its third-party urban NOA market share stood at 65%, and it had established partnerships with 24 automakers worldwide, including 9 of the global top 10 OEMs.[3][4][5]

III. Core Technology: The Flywheel & Algorithm Evolution

The “Two-Track” Product Strategy

  • Mpilot (mass-production ADAS): L2/L2+ production-ready delivery for OEMs, covering city NOA, highway NOA, and parking — the current revenue engine.
  • MSD (Momenta Self-Driving): L4 full autonomous driving R&D track powering Robotaxi/Robovan commercialization — the ultimate flywheel destination.

The Flywheel Mechanism

The flywheel’s essence is a data-closed-loop driving algorithm iteration:

  1. Mpilot fleet vehicles continuously collect real-world driving data on roads worldwide. Mpilot
  2. Data is filtered, annotated, and fed back into large model training.
  3. Improved models are pushed OTA back to production vehicles, enhancing experience.
  4. Better experience attracts more OEM partners, collecting more data — cycle repeats.

Momenta stated it once took 24 months to reach the first 100,000-unit milestone (2022), but now the same milestone can be achieved in under 40 days — a vivid demonstration of flywheel acceleration.[3]

Map-Free Architecture

Momenta adopts a map-free technology approach — a core technical enabler of its overseas strategy. HD maps in Europe and Japan involve complex licensing, data security, and real-time update regulations with prohibitive maintenance costs. The map-free system perceives the surrounding environment in real time via large models, enabling rapid deployment in new markets without local map partners.[19]

Mercedes-Benz: Highest Strategic Value International Case

In November 2024, Reuters revealed that Mercedes had selected Momenta as its ADAS supplier for 4 models launching in China between 2025 and 2027, with internal discussions about extending globally. In September 2025, both parties announced that a Momenta Flywheel Big Model-based next-generation ADAS system would debut on the all-electric CLA (China-manufactured version), covering highway, city, and parking end-to-end.[21][22][23]

BYD: Implications of the Largest Client

BYD became Momenta’s #1 revenue client in 2025 (21.6% of revenue), reflecting BYD’s urgency to rapidly augment its urban NOA capability through external partnerships. This is also the archetypal case of Momenta’s revenue shift from “development fees” to “mass-production license fees”.[15][24]

Toyota: Strategic Bridgehead to Japan

Toyota is simultaneously Momenta’s investor (Series C lead) and one of its earliest international clients. Momenta’s CEO has stated that Momenta is the first Chinese company to achieve mass-produced advanced ADAS deployment in Germany, France, Japan, and other countries.[19]

V. China’s Overseas Advanced ADAS: Momenta’s Global Path

Macro Context

China’s urban NOA commercialization scale vastly outpaces other markets:

  • January–November 2025: cumulative urban NOA-equipped passenger car sales in China: 3.219 million units, representing 15.1% of insured vehicle count. 2025
  • Among third-party suppliers, Momenta (61.06%) and Huawei HI mode (19.76%) combined account for approximately 80% of market share.
  • China is the world’s highest-density, fastest-iteration urban NOA market — an unscalable training ground for overseas competitors.

In 2026, the Global Technical Regulation on Automated Driving Systems (ADS GTR), co-led by China, the EU, UK, US, Canada, and Japan, was formally adopted — the world’s first autonomous driving standard covering all major markets, enabling “one certification, multi-country approval” and marking China’s transition into the “standard export” era.[27]

Three-Layer Overseas Architecture

Layer 1: Pre-installed in Chinese export vehicles

IM Motors (IM5/IM6) officially launched in the UK in September 2025, both equipped with Momenta Flywheel Big Model ADAS — the first milestone where Momenta’s mass-production solution reached European consumers through a Chinese vehicle brand. Momenta production solutions now span 10+ countries and regions across Asia, Europe, Oceania, Latin America, and North Africa.[28][3]

Layer 2: Supply directly to international OEMs, extend globally

The Mercedes-Benz CLA (China-manufactured) with Momenta system is in mass production; Mercedes is internally evaluating extension to European models. Renault’s partnership will bring Momenta’s city NOA to France and Spain.[22][29]

Layer 3: Robotaxi overseas (Europe, Middle East verification)

Following the strategic agreement with Uber, the first deployment began in Europe in early 2026 (with safety operators). The specific city is Munich — where Uber and Momenta plan to launch L4 Robotaxi commercial operations, with plans to expand to France and Italy. Momenta’s Robotaxi is also road-testing in Abu Dhabi and expanding in Southeast Asia through the Grab platform.[30][31][32][33][3]

Valeo Partnership: Qualitative Leap in Globalization

In September 2025, Momenta and Valeo signed a comprehensive, long-term global strategic cooperation agreement:[34]

  • Combines Valeo’s computing platforms and sensor hardware strengths with Momenta’s software algorithms
  • Jointly develops “system-level” mid-to-high-end ADAS and autonomous driving solutions covering China and overseas markets
  • Provides full lifecycle support from market development to continuous software OTA upgrades

This is the highest-level strategic integration between a Chinese software supplier and a European Tier 1 hardware leader, marking Momenta’s identity leap from “Chinese ADAS algorithm company” to “global system-level ADAS solution provider.”

VI. Key Challenges in Going Overseas

Data Compliance: Dual Pressure of GDPR and China’s Data Security Law

  • European road-test data: Pedestrian faces and license plates captured by vehicle cameras are personal data requiring anonymization under GDPR.
  • China Data Security Law: Restricts “important data” transfer overseas; European road data contains local road information, requiring careful compliance design for cross-border training use.
  • Solution: Momenta has established a local data processing center in Europe, implementing a “data stays local, capability transfers” compliant architecture.

Regulatory Entry: L3/L4 Commercialization Still Faces Fragmented Regulations

The ADS GTR is a major breakthrough, but implementation timelines vary by country. Momenta’s Munich Robotaxi must operate with safety operators and cannot go fully driverless until regulations open further. UNECE R157 and other certifications impose specific hardware layout and functional requirements that require European localization adaptation for China-tuned systems.[30][39]

At ~USD 8.9B, Momenta’s valuation is not cheap relative to Mobileye (~USD 12–15B). The profitability inflection point has not yet arrived (2025 adjusted net loss CNY 303M), and the Robotaxi business contributes a “not material” portion of total revenue.[15][13]

Structural industry assessment: China’s advanced ADAS overseas expansion is upgrading from “single-product export” to “systematic output of technology solutions + software ecosystems + data capabilities”[39][27]. The business model represented by Momenta — “software as a service + continuous OTA iteration” — is the archetypal validation pathway for Chinese automotive software going global, with the software license fee ratio rising from 3.1% to 40.1%[15] as the clearest financial signal.

Conclusion

Momenta is the most archetypal global commercialization experiment in China’s “software-defined vehicle” era. The underlying logic of its success: use China’s unparalleled mass-production data scale to train globally competitive algorithms, then deploy those algorithms to support local delivery for global OEMs — a flywheel that competitors in other countries will find extremely difficult to replicate for a considerable period.

On July 8, 2026, Momenta’s HKEX debut (first-day close +2.8% vs IPO price) makes it the third Chinese autonomous driving leader to reach public capital markets, following Pony.ai and WeRide. If the USD 8.9 billion valuation earns sustained secondary market recognition, it will send an unambiguous signal to global investors and OEMs: Chinese third-party ADAS software suppliers not only possess global competitive capability, but also have the financial foundation for sustainable public-market operations.[2]

三、核心技术：飞轮战略与算法迭代

“两条腿”产品战略

  • Mpilot（量产辅助驾驶）：面向整车厂的L2/L2+量产交付产品，覆盖城市NOA、高速NOA、泊车等全场景，是当前收入主体。
  • MSD（自动驾驶）：L4级完全自动驾驶研发路径，支撑Robotaxi/Robovan商业化，是数据飞轮的终极目标。

飞轮技术机制

“飞轮”的本质是数据闭环驱动算法迭代：

  1. Mpilot量产车在全球道路持续采集真实驾驶数据。
  2. 数据经筛选标注后反哺大模型训练。
  3. 更强大模型通过OTA推送回量产车，提升驾驶体验。
  4. 更好的体验吸引更多车企合作，采集更多数据，循环往复。

Momenta曾宣称首个10万台量产耗时24个月（2022年），而今最快不到40天即可完成——飞轮效应带来的规模加速效应显著。[3]

R6的意义:

R6通过强化学习自我博弈进化，理论上可”超越人”而非”模仿人”——类比AlphaGo从模仿棋谱到自我对弈超越人类棋手。[18]

R7的意义:

世界模型可在虚拟环境中预测行驶场景演化，大幅降低对真实道路数据的依赖，为全球不同法规下的无地图驾驶奠定基础。[13]

无高精地图方案

Momenta采用无高精地图（map-free）技术路线——这是其出海战略的核心技术支撑，高精地图在欧洲、日本等涉及复杂法规，维护成本极高；无地图方案通过大模型实时感知周边环境，可在无需本地地图合作伙伴的情况下快速部署于新市场。[19]

五、中国汽车出海高阶智驾：Momenta的全球化路径

宏观背景

中国城市NOA商业化规模远超其他国家：

  • 2025年1-11月，搭载城市NOA功能的中国乘用车销量达321.9万辆，占上险量15.1%。[25]
  • 第三方供应商中，Momenta（61.06%）和华为HI模式（19.76%）合计占约80%的市场份额。[26]
  • 中国是全球城市NOA技术密度最高、迭代最快的市场，形成了其他国家无法复制的规模化训练场。

2026年，由中国、欧盟、英国、美国、加拿大和日本共同牵头的全球自动驾驶系统技术法规（ADS GTR）正式获批——这是全球首部覆盖多主要市场的自动驾驶法规，意味着”一次认证、多国通行”，中国汽车出海进入”标准输出”新阶段。[27]

“三层并行”出海架构

第一层：随中国车企出海（量产前装）

智己汽车（IM5/IM6）于2025年9月在英国正式上市开售，均搭载Momenta飞轮大模型辅助驾驶系统——Momenta量产方案首次随中国车型进入欧洲消费者市场的里程碑。Momenta量产方案已在亚洲、欧洲、大洋洲、拉美和北非的10多个国家和地区实现落地。[3][28]

第二层：直接为国际车企在华供货，拓展至全球

奔驰CLA（中国制造）搭载Momenta方案量产，奔驰内部评估扩展至欧洲车型；雷诺合作将使Momenta方案在法国、西班牙实现城市NOA落地。[29][22]

第三层：Robotaxi出海（欧洲、中东验证）

与Uber达成战略合作后，首批于2026年初在欧洲启动（配安全员）。具体城市为慕尼黑——计划启动L4级Robotaxi商业化运营，并扩展至法国、意大利。Momenta Robotaxi同时在阿布扎比开始路测，并通过Grab平台在东南亚布局。[31][32][33][30][3]

六、出海面临的核心挑战

数据合规：双重压力

  • 欧洲路测数据：车辆摄像头采集的行人面部、车牌等属于个人数据，需按GDPR进行匿名化处理。[36]
  • 中国数据安全法：限制”重要数据”向境外传输，欧洲路测数据回传中国训练需精心设计合规路径。
  • 解决方案：Momenta已在欧洲建立本地数据处理中心，实现”数据不出境，能力可迁移”的合规架构。[37][38]

监管准入：法规碎片化

GTR是重大突破，但各成员国实施时间表不一致。Momenta慕尼黑Robotaxi必须配备安全员才能运营，真正无人化商业化仍需等待监管放开。UNECE R157等认证对硬件布局和功能设计有特定要求，中国调校系统需要欧洲本土化适配。[39][30]

Momenta的差异化优势：唯一同时具备中国超大规模量产数据积累 + 全球前十大车企客户网络 + 无地图技术路线 + 欧洲本地合规基础设施的独立第三方供应商。

估值风险

估值约USD 89亿，相较Mobileye（市值约120-150亿美元）并不便宜；盈利拐点尚未实现（2025年调整后亏损3.03亿元），Robotaxi业务对总收入贡献”并不重大”。[13][15]

行业结构性判断:

中国智驾出海正在从”单品出口”升级为”技术方案+软件生态+数据能力”的体系化输出。Momenta所代表的”软件即服务+持续OTA迭代”商业模式，软件许可费比例从3.1%提升至40.1%，是中国汽车软件走向全球的典型验证路径。[15][39][27]

结论

Momenta是中国”软件定义汽车”时代最典型的全球化商业实验。其成功的底层逻辑：用中国市场无与伦比的规模化量产数据训练出全球竞争力的算法，再以算法反哺全球车企的本地化交付——这是一个其他国家的智驾公司在相当长时间内难以复制的飞轮。

2026年7月8日在港交所上市（首日收盘较IPO价格+2.8%），Momenta成为继Pony.ai、文远知行之后，中国自动驾驶领域第三家登陆资本市场的头部企业。如果USD 89亿的估值在二级市场获得长期认可，它将向全球投资者和车企传递一个明确信号——中国第三方智驾软件供应商不仅具备全球竞争力，而且具备资本市场可持续运营的财务基础。[2]
