BYD's 792K Overseas H1 Leads a Wave of Chinese OEM Localization Bets比亚迪上半年海外79.2万辆领跑，中国车企本地化制造全面提速

BYD hit 792,256 overseas units in H1 2026 (+71% YoY) while Chery's OMODA & JAECOO surged 178% in June and three separate factory localisation moves signal a structural shift from export to local manufacturing.比亚迪2026年上半年海外累计交付79.2万辆（同比+71%），奇瑞OMODA & JAECOO 6月同比大增178%，三起本地化建厂举措同步推进，中国车企出海模式正从纯出口向本土制造转型。

By TopChinaCar Editorial作者：TopChinaCar 编辑部 · Published July 9, 2026发布于 2026-07-09 · Compiled from the primary sources cited below基于文末所列一手信源编写

Chinese automakers posted their most consequential overseas week of 2026: BYD's H1 international deliveries hit 792,256 units, already 52.8% of its full-year 1.5 million target, while Chery's OMODA & JAECOO surged 178% in a single month and three separate factory localisation announcements — in Spain, Austria and Malaysia — confirmed that tariff walls are forcing a structural shift from pure exports to local manufacturing.

BYD: Exports Become the Profitability Anchor

BYD delivered a record 175,349 vehicles overseas in June (+94.7% YoY), representing 43.5% of its total monthly sales of 403,000 units — effectively matching domestic volume for the first time. [ElectricCarsReport] The H1 cumulative figure of 792,256 units marks a 71% year-on-year rise, with Q2 alone accounting for 471,000 units (+82.5% YoY). [Ad-Hoc-News]

In Australia, BYD reached a monthly record of 18,881 units in June, closing to within 243 units of market leader Toyota. [CarNewsChina] The European manufacturing push is equally notable: the Hungary Szeged plant will begin full vehicle assembly in Q4 2026, and BYD is targeting 20,000 ultra-fast charging points across Europe by year-end, up from 7,000 today. [Ad-Hoc-News] The picture is not without shadows: Q1 net profit collapsed 55% to RMB 4.08 billion, finished-vehicle inventory exceeded RMB 160 billion, and South Korea's government EV subsidy eligibility was lost as of 1 July, forcing BYD to fund its own discounts. [Ad-Hoc-News]

Chery OMODA & JAECOO: The Fastest Electrification Inflection in Export Data

OMODA & JAECOO delivered 75,102 vehicles globally in June (+178% YoY), the second consecutive month above 75,000 units. The headline figure inside the headline: NEV models accounted for 56,188 units (+310% YoY), lifting the electrification mix to approximately 75% in a single month. [TechPlugged] The brand's H1 cumulative total now surpasses full-year 2025 output, spanning 69 core markets and 1,364 dealership showrooms — an expansion pace Chery describes as one new market every 16 days and one new store every day. [Barchart]

The European manufacturing answer is a Barcelona Zona Franca plant that will begin producing electric versions of the Omoda 5 and Jaecoo 5 in November, targeting capacity of 200,000 units per year and more than 3,000 employees by 2030. [Ara.cat] Chery's stated ambition — branded "From Million To Annual Million" — targets 1 million annual units across 108-plus countries by 2027, and Jaecoo was named Carwow's brand of the year in the UK. [Barchart]

Geely, GAC and Leapmotor: Three Models of Low-Capex Localisation

Geely posted June overseas sales that Jefferies estimates exceeded 100,000 units for the first time — a margin roughly 10 percentage points higher than domestic business. [KR-Asia] Zeekr delivered 35,169 units in June (+111% YoY) and the group is targeting 100,000-plus combined Zeekr and Lynk & Co overseas sales for the full year, with expansion into South Korea, New Zealand, South Africa and the UK planned before December. The most capital-efficient move: using Proton's existing Malaysian production lines to begin local Zeekr 7X assembly in Q1 2027. [KR-Asia]

GAC International posted H1 exports of 121,483 units (+132% YoY), nearly matching its entire 2025 export total in six months. [PR Newswire] The AION UT holds the top-selling position in pure-electric small-car segments in Hong Kong, Macau, Colombia, Uruguay and Singapore, with Australia pre-orders exceeding 600 units. The European manufacturing step came through a first AION UT prototype rolling off Magna's Austrian production line. [Yahoo Finance] Leapmotor, meanwhile, recorded its best-ever month at 93,376 global deliveries in June (+95% YoY), with overseas accounting for approximately 21,000 units. [36Kr-EN] The Stellantis Zaragoza plant's battery pack supplier entered mass-production preparation in July, with full B10 vehicle production on track for H2 2026 and more than 850 European outlets now open. [ElectricCarsReport]

XPeng, Changan and GWM: Product Offensives Across Three Continents

XPeng delivered 40,126 units globally in June (+15.9% YoY), its highest monthly figure of 2026, and is preparing its biggest overseas product event: a Munich brand launch in July to introduce the affordable Mona L03 SUV and flagship GX to European buyers across 28 countries. [EV.com] With Q1 gross margin at 20.6% and overseas revenue targeted to exceed 20% of group revenue from Q2, XPeng is simultaneously exploring additional European manufacturing options beyond its existing Magna Steyr Austria arrangement. [CnEVPost]

Changan's Deepal S05 completed a Lisbon–Madrid–Turin road delivery run marking the first European customer deliveries, sourced from its Thailand Rayong plant (capacity 100,000 units, with a planned doubling to 200,000). [Bangkok Post] Changan and BAIC signed a strategic cooperation agreement in June that explicitly covers joint overseas distribution channels — a rare SOE-level overseas-channel merger signal. [Wikipedia] GWM's June global sales came in at 108,080 units (-2.4% YoY), but overseas contributed a remarkable 56% of that total. Australia remains the focal market: nine new models are planned for 2026, including the Wey brand's first Australian launch before year-end and a target of top-five brand status. [CarExpert]

Dongfeng and FAW: SOEs Bet on European Factories and Luxury Positioning

Dongfeng's Voyah delivered 14,223 units in June (+41% YoY) and 76,264 in H1 (+36% YoY), operating across 40-plus countries with more than 240 sales and service outlets. [China Daily] The strategic anchor is a May 2026 agreement with Stellantis to form a Stellantis-led (51%) European joint venture for Voyah distribution, with local NEV production at the Stellantis Rennes plant in France. Dongfeng also unveiled its "Set Sail" global expansion strategy at the Hong Kong Auto Expo in June, committing more than RMB 100 billion over five years, with Hong Kong as the right-hand-drive strategic hub and 3,000 units already contracted for 2026. [MarketersMedia]

FAW's Hongqi opened its Singapore flagship store offering the right-hand-drive E-HS9 (120 kWh, 570 km range) and appointed Metro Group — which also represents Toyota and Mercedes-Benz — as its Thailand dealer. [Electrive] A full-year 2026 Hongqi global target of 550,000 units anchors the brand's ambition to reach 110-plus countries and 650-plus dealers by 2028. [Newsfile] Hongqi is also co-developing right-hand-drive export models with Leapmotor, with production beginning at Leapmotor's Hangzhou plant in H2 2026. [Wikipedia]

What It Means

The clearest signal from today's data is that the Chinese auto industry's overseas chapter is entering its second, harder phase. The first phase — ship cars in volume, grow fast — has delivered extraordinary headline numbers: BYD at 52.8% of a 1.5 million annual target by June, GAC matching full-year 2025 exports in six months, Chery's O&J doubling its NEV mix to 75% in a single month. The second phase, now visibly underway, is local manufacturing under tariff pressure: Leapmotor in Zaragoza, GAC via Magna in Austria, Chery in Barcelona, Dongfeng in Rennes, Geely via Proton in Malaysia, Changan via Thailand. What unites all of these is that none involves greenfield capital investment — every single bet is built on a partner's existing factory. That is rational capital discipline under uncertainty, but it also means that quality control, local-content compliance and production ramp pace are now shared risks that Chinese OEMs cannot fully control. The Changan-BAIC joint overseas channel agreement adds a further layer: Chinese SOEs are beginning to pool distribution to achieve the scale that individual brands cannot reach alone. The net result is a Chinese overseas auto industry that is simultaneously larger, more structurally embedded and more exposed to execution complexity than at any point in its history.

2026年7月，中国车企出海交出了年内最重要的一份成绩单：比亚迪上半年海外累计交付79.2万辆，已完成全年150万辆目标的52.8%；奇瑞OMODA & JAECOO单月同比大增178%；与此同时，西班牙、奥地利、马来西亚三地本地化建厂举措同步落地——关税壁垒正在将整个行业的出海模式从纯出口推向本地制造。

比亚迪：出口成为唯一盈利锚点

比亚迪6月海外单月交付创历史新高，达17.5万辆（同比+94.7%），占当月全球总销量40.3万辆的43.5%——出口占比首次逼近国内销量。[ElectricCarsReport] 上半年累计海外交付79.2万辆（同比+71%），其中Q2单季达47.1万辆（同比+82.5%）。[Ad-Hoc-News]

澳大利亚市场6月创下18,881辆的历史月度新高，与销量冠军丰田仅相差243辆。[CarNewsChina] 欧洲制造布局方面，匈牙利塞格德工厂将于Q4 2026启动整车组装，欧洲超快充桩目标年底从现有7,000座扩至20,000座。[Ad-Hoc-News] 风险同样不可忽视：Q1净利润同比暴跌55%至40.8亿元，成品车库存超1,600亿元，韩国政府EV补贴资质于7月1日起取消，比亚迪须自行提供折扣维持销售。[Ad-Hoc-News]

奇瑞OMODA & JAECOO：出口数据中最显著的电动化拐点

OMODA & JAECOO 6月全球交付75,102辆（同比+178%），连续第二个月突破75,000辆大关。更值得关注的细节是：新能源车型交付56,188辆（同比+310%），NEV占比在单月内跃升至约75%。[TechPlugged] 上半年累计销量已超过2025年全年，覆盖69个核心市场、1,364个经销商展厅，奇瑞将这一扩张速度描述为"平均每16天进入一个新市场、每天新开一家门店"。[Barchart]

应对欧盟关税的制造布局已经落地：巴塞罗那Zona Franca工厂将于11月起生产Omoda 5和Jaecoo 5电动版，计划2030年产能提升至20万辆/年、员工逾3,000人。[Ara.cat] 奇瑞将战略命名为"从百万到年百万"，目标2027年覆盖108个以上国家、年销量达100万辆，英国Jaecoo更荣获Carwow年度品牌。[Barchart]

吉利、广汽与零跑：三种低资本支出本地化路径

吉利6月海外月销量据Jefferies估计首次突破10万辆，毛利率较国内业务高约10个百分点。[KR-Asia] 极氪6月交付35,169辆（同比+111%），全年Zeekr与Lynk & Co合计海外销量目标逾10万辆，韩国、新西兰、南非、英国均计划年内进入。资本效率最高的一步是：利用宝腾现有马来西亚产线，于2027年Q1启动Zeekr 7X本地生产——这将成为极氪首个海外制造基地。[KR-Asia]

广汽国际H1出口121,483辆（同比+132%），六个月内已接近2025年全年出口量。[PR Newswire] AION UT在港澳、哥伦比亚、乌拉圭、新加坡的纯电小型车细分均位居销量第一，澳大利亚首批预售超600单；欧洲方面，首台AION UT原型车已在Magna奥地利工厂下线。[Yahoo Finance] 零跑6月全球交付93,376辆（同比+95%），创历史新高，海外贡献约2.1万辆。[36Kr-EN] Stellantis萨拉戈萨工厂B10电动SUV的电池包供应商已于7月进入量产准备阶段，整车生产预计H2 2026正式启动，欧洲网点已超850个。[ElectricCarsReport]

小鹏、长安与长城：三大洲的产品攻势

小鹏6月全球交付40,126辆（同比+15.9%），为2026年月度最高，Q1毛利率达20.6%。其年内最重大的海外产品行动即将展开：7月慕尼黑品牌发布会将正式向欧洲推出平价车型Mona L03 SUV及旗舰GX，直接挑战比亚迪和零跑在欧洲平价纯电细分的地位；目前已覆盖28个欧洲国家。[EV.com] 与此同时，小鹏正探索在现有Magna Steyr奥地利合作之外增设欧洲制造方案。[CnEVPost]

长安深蓝S05完成里斯本→马德里→都灵公路测试，标志欧洲首批客户交付正式开启，货源来自泰国Rayong工厂（现有产能10万辆/年，计划翻倍至20万辆）。[Bangkok Post] 6月，长安与北汽签署战略合作协议，明确涵盖共建海外销售渠道——这是中国国有车企协同出海的罕见信号。[Wikipedia] 长城6月全球销量108,080辆（同比-2.4%），但海外贡献高达56%，约60,500辆。澳大利亚依然是核心阵地：2026年计划推出9款新车，并将在年底前完成Wey品牌首次澳洲亮相，目标跻身当地前五大品牌。[CarExpert]

东风与一汽：国有车企押注欧洲工厂与豪华定位

岚图6月交付14,223辆（同比+41%），H1累计76,264辆（同比+36%），已在40余个国家运营，拥有240余个销售服务网点。[China Daily] 战略锚点是2026年5月签订的与Stellantis合作协议——双方将在欧洲组建由Stellantis控股（51%）的合资公司负责岚图分销，并在法国雷恩工厂实现东风NEV本地化生产。东风还于6月香港车展发布"扬帆计划"全球扩张战略，承诺未来五年投入逾1,000亿元，以香港为右舵市场战略枢纽，2026年已签3,000辆订单。[MarketersMedia]

一汽红旗新加坡旗舰店正式开业，主推右舵版E-HS9（120 kWh/570 km续航），并任命同时代理丰田和奔驰的Metro Group为泰国经销商。[Electrive] 2026年红旗全年全球销量目标55万辆，远期目标2028年覆盖110余个国家、650余家经销商。[Newsfile] 红旗还与零跑联合开发右舵出口车型，H2 2026将在零跑杭州工厂投产——这是一汽借助外部伙伴加速进入右舵市场的捷径。[Wikipedia]

深层解读

今日数据最清晰的信号，是中国汽车出海已进入更难打的第二阶段。第一阶段——大批量出口、快速增长——交出了亮眼答卷：比亚迪6月底已完成全年目标52.8%，广汽六个月追平2025全年出口量，奇瑞O&J单月NEV占比飙至75%。而第二阶段正在眼前展开——关税压力下的本地化制造：零跑在萨拉戈萨、广汽借道Magna在奥地利、奇瑞在巴塞罗那、东风在雷恩、吉利借宝腾在马来西亚、长安借泰国工厂辐射欧洲。这些布局有一个共同特征：没有一家选择新建绿地工厂，全部依托合作伙伴的现有设施。这是不确定性环境下理性的资本纪律，但也意味着质量管控、本地化内容达标与产能爬坡速度，成为中国车企无法完全掌控的共担风险。长安与北汽联合共建海外渠道的协议更增添了一个新维度：国有车企开始抱团布局，以单一品牌无法实现的规模共同参与海外市场竞争。综合来看，中国汽车出海产业的体量更大、结构性嵌入更深，同时所面临的执行复杂度，也达到了历史最高点。
